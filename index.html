15.1 Introduction

Evaluating Systems in Real Settings

Imagine that you have designed a new app to help a class keep track of a shared pet. The app will schedule when the hamster goes home with each student, remind families when to feed it, and record notes about its care. Both teachers and parents can see the schedule and send messages to each other.

To understand whether this app works well in practice, you need to see how people actually use it. Do families understand the schedule? Do students remember to check it? Is the interface easy to read on phones as well as laptops? These questions are not always answered by lab studies alone.
